# The Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilization flourished from about 2600 to 1900 BCE along the
Indus River and its tributaries. Its two largest cities were Harappa and
Mohenjo-daro, and the culture is therefore also called the Harappan
civilization.

Harappan cities are remarkable for their planning: a grid layout of streets,
standardized fired bricks, covered drainage, and sophisticated water management
including the Great Bath at Mohenjo-daro. This level of urban engineering was
unmatched in the contemporary world.

The civilization had a writing system, the Indus script, which remains
undeciphered to this day. Around 1900 BCE the cities declined, likely due to a
combination of shifting river courses, climate change, and weakened trade.
