# Religions of South Asia

South Asia is the birthplace of several major world religions. Hinduism is the
oldest, a diverse tradition with no single founder, drawing on the ancient Vedas
and emphasizing concepts such as dharma, karma, and rebirth.

Buddhism was founded in the fifth century BCE by Siddhartha Gautama, the
Buddha, who taught the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path as a way to end
suffering. Jainism arose in the same period, teaching radical nonviolence, or
ahimsa, toward all living things.

Islam arrived later, spreading through trade along the coasts and through
conquest in the north from the eighth century onward. Sikhism emerged in the
Punjab in the fifteenth century under Guru Nanak, teaching devotion to one God
and the equality of all people.
