# The Mughal Empire

The Mughal Empire was founded in 1526 when Babur, a descendant of Timur and
Genghis Khan, defeated the Delhi Sultanate at the First Battle of Panipat. At
its height the empire controlled most of the subcontinent.

The emperor Akbar, who ruled from 1556 to 1605, is considered the greatest
Mughal. He expanded the empire and is remembered for his policy of religious
tolerance: he abolished the jizya tax on non-Muslims and sought dialogue among
faiths. His administration organized the empire through the mansabdari system,
a ranked bureaucracy of officials.

Later emperors are known for monumental architecture. Shah Jahan built the Taj
Mahal at Agra as a tomb for his wife Mumtaz Mahal. The empire declined in the
eighteenth century after the long, costly wars of Aurangzeb.
