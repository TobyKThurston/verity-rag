# British colonial rule

The British East India Company began as a trading enterprise but gradually
seized political control, decisively after the Battle of Plassey in 1757, which
gave it power over Bengal. The Company governed vast territories through a mix
of direct rule and alliances with local princes.

In 1857 a widespread uprising, often called the Sepoy Mutiny or the First War
of Independence, challenged Company rule. After suppressing it, the British
Crown took direct control in 1858, beginning the period known as the British
Raj.

Colonial economic policy reoriented the economy toward British interests:
deindustrializing the once-dominant Indian textile sector, prioritizing cash
crops, and extracting revenue through heavy land taxes. Recurrent famines under
British administration killed millions.
