# Independence and Partition

The Indian independence movement gathered force in the twentieth century. Mohandas
Gandhi led mass campaigns of nonviolent civil disobedience, or satyagraha,
including the 1930 Salt March against the British salt tax. Jawaharlal Nehru
became the movement's leading political figure and India's first prime minister.

As independence approached, the Muslim League under Muhammad Ali Jinnah demanded
a separate state for Muslims. In 1947 the British partitioned the subcontinent
into two independent nations: a secular but Hindu-majority India and a
Muslim-majority Pakistan.

Partition triggered one of the largest mass migrations in history, as some
fifteen million people crossed the new borders, accompanied by horrific
communal violence that killed hundreds of thousands.
